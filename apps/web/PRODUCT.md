# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Personal trainers in Brazil who manage multiple clients' workout routines. They're busy professionals working in-person or remotely, who need to:
- Create and assign customized workout routines (AB, ABC, ABCDE splits)
- Track student progress over time
- Manage exercise libraries with media references
- View dashboards that summarize their student and routine status at a glance

They are often on the go, switching between gym floor and phone/tablet, so speed and clarity matter more than visual flair.

## Product Purpose

A complete platform for personal trainers to manage workout routines, track student evolution, and maximize results. It reduces the admin burden of routine creation and progress tracking so trainers can focus on coaching.

## Brand Personality

Clean, Modern, Energetic. The brand communicates professional confidence without being aggressive or brooding. It feels like a capable tool, not a gym poster.

## Anti-references

Generic fitness apps — avoid overly aggressive gym aesthetics, heavy gradients, dark brooding tones, or anything that prioritizes "pump" over professionalism. This is a tool for coaches, not a supplement brand.

## Design Principles

1. **Clear before clever** — readability and scanability come first. Trainers should find what they need in one glance, not one puzzle.
2. **Dark but not dramatic** — the dark UI is functional (reduces glare on the gym floor, makes accent cues pop), not atmospheric. Avoid moody gradients or glass effects.
3. **Progress is the product** — student evolution data should be the visual hero of every screen that has it. Metrics, streaks, and goals should feel motivating, not like a spreadsheet.
4. **Speak the trainer's language** — Portuguese-first terminology, professional tone, domain-native labels. No fitness-bro slang.
5. **One task, one focus** — every screen has a primary action. Forms and tables should not compete with chrome or decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum — body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, keyboard-navigable, visible focus indicators, semantic HTML. Accent color (#AF9150) should pass contrast checks against all backgrounds it appears on.
